Lohagad Fort: The Iron Fortress

History

Lohagad, which literally means "Iron Fort," is one of the many hill forts of Maharashtra. Situated near the hill station of Lonavala, it was a strategic post for many dynasties, including the Satavahanas, Chalukyas, and Rashtrakutas. Under Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, it was used to store treasury looted from Surat. Its strong walls remain virtually "iron-clad" and unconquered to this day.

Major Attractions

  • 🚩 Vinchu Kata: A long and narrow wall-like structure resembling a scorpion's tail.
  • 🚩 Four Massive Gates: Ganesh, Narayan, Hanuman, and Maha Darwaja.
  • 🚩 Lohagadwadi: The base village offering authentic Maharashtrian culture.
  • 🚩 Bhairav Caves: Ancient Buddhist caves located nearby.

Visitor's Guide

Best Time: Monsoon (July to September) for lush greenery
Difficulty: Easy (Perfect for beginners)
Distance: 65 km from Pune City
