Sinhagad Fort: The Lion's Bastion

History

Originally known as Kondhana, Sinhagad Fort has a history dating back 2,000 years. It is most famous for the Battle of Sinhagad in 1670, where Tanaji Malusare, a commander for Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, scaled the steep cliffs to recapture the fort. His bravery inspired the famous quote: "Gad ala, pan Sinha gela" (The fort is captured, but the Lion is lost).

Major Attractions

  • Pune & Kalyan Darwaja: Massive entrance gates showcasing Maratha architecture.
  • Tanaji Malusare Samadhi: A memorial dedicated to the legendary warrior.
  • Dev टाके (Dev Take): Ancient rock-cut cisterns that provide sweet water all year round.
  • Hawa Punja: A scenic point known for its constant refreshing breeze.

Visitor's Guide

Best Time: Monsoon (July-Sept) or Winter (Oct-Feb)
Timings: 5:00 AM to 6:00 PM
Food: Must try Pithla-Bhakri and Fresh Curd (Dahi)
